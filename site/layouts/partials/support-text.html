The Civil Rights Act of 1964 offers protections for workers from various forms of discrimination by their employers, including discrimination on the basis of:

  • Race
  • Color
  • National origin
  • Religion
  • Sex
  • Sexual orientation

Additionally, both the federal government and the state of California have various laws that offer further protections within these categories, as well as in others such as age. When employers violate anti-discrimination laws, they can be held legally accountable and forced to not only stop their unfair practices, but also to pay damages to any affected employees.

If you feel that your employer has treated you unfairly because of your sex, race, age, etc., (i.e., they used your status as part of their decision to underpay you, deny you a promotion, fire you, etc.), you should seek legal counsel immediately.
