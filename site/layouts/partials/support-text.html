Speak To The Best Workplace Retaliation Lawyers

Retaliation occurs when an employer takes steps to punish an employee or job application for exercising their rights to be free of workplace discrimination. Workers in California are protected by both federal law (the U.S. Equal Opportunity Commision) and a number of state laws and legal provisions.

Under these laws, employers are prohibited from retaliating against workers or applicants for reasons such as the following:

  • Reporting employment discrimination (including harassment) to a manager or supervisor
  • Initiating or witnessing a discrimination complaint, charge, lawsuit, or investigation
  • Cooperating with an investigation about alleged harassment
  • Refusing to comply with orders that would lead to an act of discrimination
  • Asking for accommodations based one’s disability or religion
  • Inquiring about salary information in the workplace in order to identify the possibility of discriminatory wages
  • Refusing to sign certain agreements with the employer
  • Reporting illegal activity by the employer to a public agency
  • Filing a workers’ compensation claim

Acts of retaliation can take the form of an unfair termination, demotion, reduction in pay, transfer, as well as other activities such as spreading rumors about the employee or applicant, or giving a worker a performance review that is lower than deserved.
